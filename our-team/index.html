About Us!

Jorge Galvez Mendez

Jorge is a student at the Mid-Career Master in Public Administration at Harvard Kennedy School. He has 16+years of experience in international development at the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) and in his native country, Mexico. Jorge has done extensive work in the areas of investment and small business development in emerging and developing countries.

Justin Jensen

Justin is a dual-degree student at Harvard Kennedy School (Master of Public Administration) and The Wharton School (Master of Business Administration, Finance). Prior to this, he received a Masters degree in Economic Development from the University of Cambridge. His work experience includes management consulting, investment banking, and private equity in the US, Europe, Middle East, and Africa. His passions include snowboarding, creative writing, and... sushi.

Manuel Sada Vazquez

Manuel has more than 10 years of experience in the public sector, in the field of multilateral, regional and community diplomacy. Prior to Harvard's Kennedy School, he served as Director of Outreach at the Institute for Mexicans Abroad of the Foreign Ministry of Mexico, Deputy Director General for Latin America and the Caribbean and advisor for human rights and political affairs at the Permanent Mission of Mexico to the UN. He holds a B.A. in International Relations from the University of Illinois and an M.A. in International Relations from the University of Chicago.
